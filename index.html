Intro

Many people come to therapy feeling disconnected from themselves, their relationships, or the life they are living. I help people feel more able to live with choice, meaning, and authenticity.

What first drew me to study psychology as an adolescent was a desire for self-understanding and for finding a way to be at ease with life as it is. I discovered early on how powerful it can be to be deeply listened to. Being seen in this way often sits at the heart of connection and interdependence.

Therapeutic Perspective

I help people discover meaning

Amor Fati Psychotherapy adopts an existential-phenomenological lens, with particular attention to authenticity and responsibility as methods of discovery. This approach supports clients in making sense of their personal trajectory and in orienting themselves toward a life that feels more intentional and meaningful.

Friedrich Nietzsche’s concept of amor fati—the love of one’s fate—speaks to the courage of embracing circumstances we did not necessarily choose for ourselves. Despite significant personal hardship, Nietzsche emphasised living creatively and courageously, believing that the challenges inherent in existence can be met as expressions of freedom and as pathways toward meaning and purpose.

I practise from a pluralistic framework, drawing on different therapeutic approaches depending on what you are seeking from therapy. Having worked across private practice, community services, and in-patient settings, I am experienced in supporting people through both acute distress and longer-term therapeutic work.

I have over a decade of professional psychological and therapeutic experience working internationally. My work has included assessing and supporting children as young as five, and working with families, adolescents, and adults across a range of settings.

My practice integrates the following modalities:

  • Existential-Phenomenological
  • Cognitive Behavioural Therapy (CBT)
  • Psychodynamic
  • Compassion Focused Theray
